Fighter's Nutrition Guide

Fight Week Nutrition

7 days out: Start reducing carbs gradually. Increase water to 4-5L/day.

3 days out: Low carb, moderate protein, minimal sodium. Water load.

Weigh-in day: Sip water only. Post weigh-in: rehydrate with electrolytes, eat easily digestible carbs.

Fight day: Light meals 3-4 hours before. Banana + honey 1 hour before. Sip water throughout.

Budget Fighter Staples

Oats, eggs, chicken thighs, rice, frozen veg, tinned tuna, peanut butter, bananas, sweet potatoes, Greek yoghurt. Under £25/week if you shop smart.

Macros for Fighters

Protein: 2-2.5g per kg (muscle repair + power)

Carbs: 4-6g per kg on training days (fuel)

Fat: 0.8-1g per kg (hormones + joints)

Fighter's Training Principles

The Hammer Hands Method

Power comes from the ground up. Legs, hips, core, then hands. If your legs are weak, your punches are weak.

Fight Camp Structure

Weeks 6-4: High volume. Build your base. Strength, cardio, sparring.

Weeks 3-2: Peak intensity. Hard sparring, speed work, tactical drilling.

Week 1: Taper down. Light work, stay sharp, make weight, rest.

Non-Negotiables

Roadwork 3x/week minimum. Bag work daily. Sparring 2x/week in camp. Core every session. Stretch after every session — injuries end careers.

Recovery Protocol

Fighter's Recovery Essentials

The 48-Hour Rule

After a fight or hard sparring: 48 hours minimum before any impact training. Your brain needs time. This is non-negotiable.

Daily Recovery Stack

Sleep: 8-9 hours. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep.

Hydration: 3-4L water daily. More during camp.

Nutrition: Protein within 30 mins of training. Carbs to refuel.

Mobility: 10 mins stretching after every session.

Hand Care for Fighters

Ice hands after heavy bag work. Wrap properly every time. If knuckles swell — rest, don't push through. Hands are your career.
